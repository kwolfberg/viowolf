About

Kaitlin Wolfberg is a versatile violinist, violist, and string arranger based in Burbank, California. A graduate of Berklee College of Music, she has been an active contributor to the Los Angeles music scene for over a decade. Her work spans various genres, including pop, rock, Americana, and indie.


Wolfberg’s impressive résumé includes performances with renowned artists and groups such as H.E.R., Los Lobos, and The Lemon Twigs. She has also collaborated with members of The Band, Big Star, The Lovin’ Spoonful, and The Beach Boys. Notably, she performed at the Grammy Awards with H.E.R. and has toured as an opening act for Brian Wilson. Her string arrangements have been featured in projects for The Monkees and Alice Wallace, among others.


In addition to her performance and arranging work, Wolfberg is a sought-after session musician. She has contributed to recordings by artists like Emitt Rhodes, Dweezil Zappa, and Miranda Lee Richards. Her home studio setup allows her to provide high-quality remote recordings, offering services that range from solo violin and viola parts to full string arrangements.


Beyond her collaborative efforts, Wolfberg has released her own music, showcasing her talents as a composer and performer. Her tracks, such as “Car Ride Through A Foreign Land” and “Hello Sweet Goodbye,” highlight her ability to blend classical sensibilities with contemporary styles.


Wolfberg’s dedication to her craft and her collaborative spirit have made her a respected figure in the music community. Her contributions continue to enrich a wide array of musical projects, reflecting her passion and versatility as an artist.
